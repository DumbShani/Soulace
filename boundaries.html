Boundaries.

Boundaries refer to the personal limits we establish to safeguard our emotional, mental, and physical health. They clarify what behaviors we find acceptable and what we do not, influencing how we permit others to treat us and how we care for ourselves. Boundaries can be emotional (to protect your feelings), physical (related to personal space), time-related (concerning our availability), or mental (involving respect for our thoughts and opinions). Remember: Boundaries are not walls to keep people out; they are fences that help you feel safe while maintaining healthy connections.

•⁠ ⁠Promote Self-Respect - Boundaries allow you to focus on your own needs and uphold your values, which in turn boosts your self-esteem. •⁠ ⁠Prevent Overwhelm and Burnout - They help maintain balance by preventing you from taking on too much. •⁠ ⁠Strengthen Relationships - Boundaries set clear expectations, encouraging mutual respect and minimizing conflicts. •⁠ ⁠Empower Emotional Health - They enable you to manage your energy, safeguard your peace, and take charge of your feelings without being affected by others' emotions.

•⁠ ⁠Identity Your Limits - Take some time to think about what situations make you teel uncomfortable or drained. Notice how you emotionally react in these scenarios. •⁠ ⁠Communicate Clearly and Calmly - Use "I" statements to share your needs. For example: "I need some time to recharge after work, so I won't be available to chat until later." •⁠ ⁠Learn to Say No - Remember that saying no is a complete sentence. Practice doing it without feeling guilty. •⁠ ⁠Be Consistent - Maintaining firm and consistent boundaries helps others understand and respect your limits. •⁠ ⁠Set Consequences When Necessary - If someone keeps crossing your boundaries, calmly enforce the consequences you've previously communicated.
